# Enterprise Support Documentation

## Overview

This document outlines the enterprise support framework for the Risk Assessment Service, including support tiers, service level agreements, escalation procedures, and customer success management.

## Support Tiers

### 1. Support Levels

#### Standard Support
- **Availability**: Business hours (9 AM - 5 PM, Monday-Friday)
- **Response Time**: 4 hours for critical issues, 24 hours for standard issues
- **Communication**: Email and ticket system
- **Coverage**: Basic support and issue resolution
- **Pricing**: Included with basic service plan

#### Premium Support
- **Availability**: Extended hours (8 AM - 8 PM, Monday-Friday)
- **Response Time**: 2 hours for critical issues, 8 hours for standard issues
- **Communication**: Email, ticket system, and phone support
- **Coverage**: Enhanced support with dedicated account manager
- **Pricing**: Additional $500/month

#### Enterprise Support
- **Availability**: 24/7 support coverage
- **Response Time**: 1 hour for critical issues, 4 hours for standard issues
- **Communication**: Email, ticket system, phone, and dedicated Slack channel
- **Coverage**: Full support with dedicated technical account manager
- **Pricing**: Additional $2,000/month

## Escalation Procedures

### 1. Escalation Levels

#### Level 1 - Initial Response
- **Response Time**: Immediate to 1 hour
- **Team**: First-line support team
- **Responsibilities**: Initial assessment, basic troubleshooting, issue categorization
- **Escalation Criteria**: Issues requiring specialized knowledge or higher authority

#### Level 2 - Technical Escalation
- **Response Time**: 1-4 hours
- **Team**: Technical support team
- **Responsibilities**: Advanced troubleshooting, technical analysis, solution implementation
- **Escalation Criteria**: Issues requiring engineering team or management involvement

#### Level 3 - Engineering Escalation
- **Response Time**: 4-8 hours
- **Team**: Engineering team
- **Responsibilities**: Code-level analysis, bug fixes, system modifications
- **Escalation Criteria**: Issues requiring product team or executive involvement

#### Level 4 - Executive Escalation
- **Response Time**: 8-24 hours
- **Team**: Executive team
- **Responsibilities**: Strategic decisions, resource allocation, customer relationship management
- **Escalation Criteria**: Issues affecting multiple customers or business-critical systems

### 2. Escalation Triggers

#### Automatic Escalation
- **SLA Breach**: Automatic escalation when SLA is breached
- **Critical Issues**: Automatic escalation for critical issues
- **Multiple Customers Affected**: Automatic escalation when multiple customers are affected
- **Security Incidents**: Automatic escalation for security incidents
- **Data Loss**: Automatic escalation for data loss incidents

#### Manual Escalation
- **Customer Request**: Manual escalation at customer request
- **Support Team Decision**: Manual escalation by support team
- **Management Decision**: Manual escalation by management
- **Technical Complexity**: Manual escalation for technically complex issues
- **Business Impact**: Manual escalation for high business impact issues

### 3. Escalation Process

#### Escalation Steps
1. **Issue Assessment**: Assess the severity and impact of the issue
2. **Escalation Decision**: Decide whether escalation is necessary
3. **Escalation Notification**: Notify relevant stakeholders of escalation
4. **Resource Allocation**: Allocate appropriate resources for resolution
5. **Progress Monitoring**: Monitor progress and provide updates
6. **Resolution Verification**: Verify resolution and customer satisfaction
7. **Post-Escalation Review**: Conduct post-escalation review and improvement

#### Escalation Communication
- **Internal Communication**: Internal team communication during escalation
- **Customer Communication**: Customer communication during escalation
- **Status Updates**: Regular status updates during escalation
- **Resolution Communication**: Resolution communication and follow-up
- **Post-Escalation Communication**: Post-escalation communication and lessons learned
